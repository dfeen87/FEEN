## 2. The Fallacy of Linear Fragility in Skeletal Medicine

Modern pharmacological interventions rely heavily on linear logic—the assumption that flooding the systemic environment with a chemical compound will reliably reach and repair a degraded target. This "spray and pray" methodology results in high toxicity, low efficacy, and biological "noise." It ignores the physical laws of network theory.

Bone is not a static object; it is an active, self-reconfiguring biological mesh. Treating a bone tumor or metabolic degradation as an isolated "broken part" guarantees failure because the pathology is fundamentally integrated into the host's topological network. Disease does not shatter the system immediately; it degrades the network gracefully by hijacking local routing protocols. To "crush" these pathologies, we must stop targeting the node and begin targeting the network's adjacency matrix.
